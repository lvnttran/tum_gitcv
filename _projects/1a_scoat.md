This project spans two phases, from an early community-service prototype to a refined, field-ready product.

### Phase 1 — Versatile Overcoat (Apr 2019 – Oct 2019)

As part of **Engineering Projects in Community Service (EPICS)** at Da Nang University of Science and Technology, a multidisciplinary, vertically-integrated student team (freshmen through seniors, working across up to seven semesters) designed a first prototype: a **versatile overcoat** intended for fishermen working in difficult weather conditions.

### Phase 2 — sCoat & sSIM, by Next-In (Feb 2021 – Nov 2021)

As part of **Maker to Entrepreneur (MEP)** initiative, the overcoat concept was developed further into **sCoat**, a combination overcoat and life jacket. Using a gas-control system and a buoy system, sCoat can support buoyancy for over 72 hours in floating conditions.

Alongside it, **sSIM** is a companion locate-and-identify system that works like a flight recorder, reporting a fisherman's location in an emergency.
