In new-product Research & Development (R&D) projects, decision-making is a complex process involving many stakeholders — researchers, users, and investors — who often struggle to reach mutual agreement due to conflicting expectations, demands, and priorities. This is a major source of uncertainty in the decision-making process.

**DeMas** is a tool that supports this process using a multi-criteria method involving multiple stakeholders, in which consensus is promoted by the project manager and by the tool itself — identifying points of conflict and tracking how they evolve across several rounds of discussion. The goal is to shorten decision-making time and provide feedback as suggestions, helping participants adjust their preferences during discussion in order to reach consensus.

### Development phases

- **Phase 1 (Jun 2020 – Dec 2020, Da Nang University of Science and Technology):** Django web application implementing the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) for R&D project consensus-building.
- **Phase 2 (Dec 2024 – Jun 2025, extension):** added Random Forest–based support for decisions involving uncertain constraints.
